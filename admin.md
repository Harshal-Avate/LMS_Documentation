# **1. Login**

The secure entry portal for administering the LMS platform.

## **1.1 How to Access**

- Open your web browser and navigate to the admin portal URL provided by
  your institution (e.g., https://lms.simplesphere.in/login).

## **1.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **Username Input Field** --- Box to enter your registered
  administrative username.

- **Password Input Field** --- Box to type your password, complete with
  a show/hide toggle (eye icon).

- **Stay Logged In Checkbox** --- Selectable option to persist your
  admin session across browser instances.

- **Sign In Button** --- Primary action button to authenticate
  credentials.

- **Forgot Password? Link** --- Recovery pathway to reset credentials.

- **Role-Based Redirect System** --- Backend router that automatically
  direct users to /admin or /mentor workspaces based on credentials.

## **1.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Open the login page in your web browser.

Step 2 -- Enter your assigned Username in the first field.

Step 3 -- Type your Password in the second field (click the eye icon to
verify spelling).

Step 4 -- (Optional) Check \'Stay logged in\' to remain signed in.

Step 5 -- Click the \'Sign In\' button. The system will process your
credentials and redirect you to the Admin Dashboard.

> **📌 Note:** Admin roles are pre-configured. If you attempt to log in
> using student credentials, the router will redirect you to the student
> portal instead of the admin dashboard.

# **2. Admin Dashboard**

The central control deck displaying operational highlights and
system-wide metrics.

## **2.1 How to Access**

- After logging in, you will land here automatically.

- Alternatively, click \'Overview\' under the CORE group in the left
  sidebar menu.

## **2.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **Center Switcher Dropdown** --- Filter located in the header to view
  data for specific centers.

- **Overview Stat Cards** --- High-level metrics displaying Total
  Students, Total Mentors, Total Courses, and Collections (Revenue).

- **Enrollment Trends Graph** --- Interactive charts mapping student
  sign-ups over time.

- **Center Performance Leaderboard** --- Summary of student counts and
  quality scores by center.

- **Recent Leads List** --- Table showing newly registered leads.

- **System Activity Feed** --- Logs of recent administrative changes.

## **2.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Locate the Center Switcher dropdown in the top bar.

Step 2 -- Click the dropdown to view the list of authorized educational
centers.

Step 3 -- Select a center to dynamically filter all dashboard statistics
and metrics.

> **📌 Note:** Super Admins can toggle and view data across all centers,
> while Center Admins are restricted to their specific assigned nodes.

# **3. User Management**

The workspace to manage platform personnel, access privileges, and
educational centers.

## **3.1 How to Access**

- To manage students, select \'Students\' under the CORE group in the
  left sidebar.

- To manage mentors, select \'Mentor Hub\' under the CORE group.

- To manage roles, select \'Access Control\' under the SYSTEM group.

## **3.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **Student/Mentor Registry Table** --- Searchable lists containing
  names, contact info, and status tags (Active, Completed, Dropped).

- **Create/Add User Button** --- Action button triggering a popup form
  to register new accounts.

- **Edit Action Icon** --- Pencil icon in each row to modify email,
  name, center, and role.

- **Deactivate/Delete Icon** --- Trash can icon or toggle switch to
  revoke access.

- **Upload Vault** --- Document section on detail pages to verify
  student ID proofs or credentials.

## **3.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Navigate to the Students page under the CORE menu group.

Step 2 -- Click the \'Add Student\' button to open the registry form.

Step 3 -- Enter Name, 10-digit Phone, Email, select the assigned Center,
and set tuition details (Total Fees, Paid, Balance).

Step 4 -- Click \'Save\' to activate the account.

Step 5 -- To deactivate a user, search their name in the table, click
edit, and toggle the status to \'Dropped\' or click the deactivate
button.

> **📌 Note:** Validation rules are strictly enforced: student phone
> numbers must contain exactly 10 digits, and fees cannot be negative.

# **4. Batch Management**

The panel to select courses and analyze detailed student learning
metrics.

## **4.1 How to Access**

- Select \'Course Students\' option under the ACADEMICS group in the
  left sidebar menu.

## **4.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **Course Selector Dropdown** --- Filters student cards by their active
  course/batch.

- **Batch Analytics Overview** --- Quick statistics showing enrolled
  student counts.

- **Student Grid** --- List of cards for each enrolled student
  displaying Lecture Progress and Assignment Progress bars.

- **Details Popup** --- Modal displaying quiz ratings and date of
  enrollment.

- **Batch Assignment Modals** --- Popups to add students to courses.

## **4.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Click the Course Selector dropdown at the top of the page.

Step 2 -- Choose the course you want to audit. The grid will instantly
populate with student cards.

Step 3 -- Click on a student\'s card to open their detailed Analytics
Modal.

Step 4 -- Review their statistics and click the redirect button to view
them in the main students panel.

> **📌 Note:** If a student\'s progress falls behind, you can click the
> redirect button in their detail card to open their profile and contact
> them.

# **5. Course Management**

The catalog tool to create, edit, structure, and assign mentors to
courses.

## **5.1 How to Access**

- Select \'Course Hub\' under the ACADEMICS group in the left sidebar
  menu.

## **5.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **Course Table** --- Grid displaying thumbnails, course levels, price,
  visibility settings, and enrolled student counts.

- **Create Course Button** --- Action button to add a new course.

- **Search & Filter Panel** --- Input boxes to filter courses by
  category and status.

- **Course Form Modal** --- Form displaying course title, description,
  level, price, visibility, prerequisites, learning outcomes, and mentor
  assignments.

## **5.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Click the \'Create Course\' button at the top of the page.

Step 2 -- Fill in the Course Title, Description, and select a Category.

Step 3 -- Select the course Level (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced) and
enter the duration.

Step 4 -- Toggle the \'Is Paid\' switch and set a price.

Step 5 -- Select the assigned Mentors from the dropdown.

Step 6 -- Click \'Save\' to register the course.

> **📌 Note:** Only courses marked as \"Published\" will be visible in
> the student panel. Draft courses remain hidden during content
> creation.

# **6. Announcements**

The communications platform to create, pin, and target administrative
notices.

## **6.1 How to Access**

- Select \'Announcements\' under the CORE group in the left sidebar
  menu.

## **6.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **New Announcement Button** --- Form trigger to compose a notice.

- **Priority Filters** --- Buttons to filter feed by Urgent, High,
  Medium, or Low priority.

- **Pinned Section** --- Top-anchored feed displaying pinned
  announcements.

- **Announcement Cards** --- Visual cards displaying title, description,
  color-coded priority badge, audience tag, creator name, role, and
  timestamp.

- **Edit/Delete Actions** --- Control options visible on cards author is
  authorized to manage.

## **6.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Click the \'New Announcement\' button to open the form.

Step 2 -- Enter the Title and compile the Message content in the text
area.

Step 3 -- Set the Priority level and select the target Audience
(Everyone, Students Only, Mentors Only).

Step 4 -- Check the \'Pin this announcement\' checkbox if you want to
keep it at the top of the feed.

Step 5 -- Click \'Post Announcement\' to publish the notice immediately.

> **📌 Note:** Pinned announcements are critical for highlighting exams,
> holiday notices, or major system upgrades.

# **7. Assessments Management**

The Kanban board interface to assign tasks and track mentor
deliverables.

## **7.1 How to Access**

- Select \'Tasks\' under the CORE group in the left sidebar menu.

## **7.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **Assign Task Button** --- Opens the task creation form modal.

- **Kanban Columns** --- Four statuses: TO DO, IN PROGRESS, REVIEW,
  DONE.

- **Task Cards** --- Visual representations of tasks displaying title,
  description, priority badge, and assigned mentor.

- **Task Form Modal** --- Inputs for Title, Description, Assigned
  Mentor, and Priority.

## **7.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Click the \'Assign Task\' button at the top of the page.

Step 2 -- Input the Task Title and compile instructions in the
Description field.

Step 3 -- Select the target Mentor from the dropdown list.

Step 4 -- Choose a priority level (Low, Medium, High).

Step 5 -- Click \'Create Task\' to add the card to the TO DO column.

Step 6 -- Drag and drop cards across columns to update task progress.

> **📌 Note:** Drag and drop task cards across the Kanban board columns
> to update their progress state in real-time.

# **8. Attendance Management**

The tracker to audit daily attendance, export logs, and adjust records.

## **8.1 How to Access**

- Select \'Attendance\' under the CORE group in the left sidebar menu.

## **8.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **Summary KPI Cards** --- Today\'s Present, Today\'s Absent,
  Attendance Marked, and Avg. Attendance metrics.

- **Import/Export Buttons** --- Quick commands to upload bulk records
  via CSV or export data in Excel format.

- **Student Registry Table** --- Registry list displaying student names,
  course names, and attendance percentage progress bars.

- **View Calendar Button** --- Direct link in each student row opening
  their calendar view.

- **Individual Calendar Grid** --- Displays daily checks/crosses
  (Present/Absent) with manual toggle buttons and override options.

## **8.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Locate the student in the registry table using the search
input.

Step 2 -- Click the \'View Calendar\' button in their row.

Step 3 -- In the calendar grid, navigate to the target month using the
arrow keys.

Step 4 -- On the desired date, click the Green Checkmark to mark
Present, or the Red X to mark Absent.

Step 5 -- Click the trash bin icon to clear a daily record.

> **📌 Note:** Bulk attendance data can be imported by clicking \"Import
> Records\" and uploading a CSV template containing student IDs, dates,
> and statuses.

# **9. Analytics & Reports**

The AI-powered interface for natural language query processing and LMS
reports.

## **9.1 How to Access**

- Select \'AI Insights\' option under the ACADEMICS group in the left
  sidebar menu.

## **9.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **AI Tool Selector Cards** --- Five dedicated models: Admin Assistant,
  Attendance Insights, Document Q&A, Lead Intelligence, and Accounts
  Advisor.

- **Chat Console** --- Input area to enter search prompts and view
  analysis transcripts.

- **KPI Cards** --- Total run queries, feature count, and engine speed
  indicators.

- **Suggest Prompts** --- Quick template query buttons based on the
  active tool card.

## **9.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Select one of the AI tool cards (e.g., \'Accounts Advisor\').

Step 2 -- Click a suggested query button or type a custom question in
the chat bar.

Step 3 -- Click the \'Process\' button to send your request.

Step 4 -- Review the generated text report and structured metadata.

> **📌 Note:** The AI assistant retrieves real-time statistics from the
> Django database, providing instant insight without manual spreadsheet
> generation.

# **10. Certificates**

The Certificate Issuer console for generating course credentials.

## **10.1 How to Access**

- Select \'Certification\' under the ACADEMICS group in the left sidebar
  menu.

## **10.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **Credential Issuer Form** --- Inputs for Student Name, Course Title,
  Duration (defaults to 03 MONTHS), Certificate ID, Start Date, and End
  Date.

- **Generate Button** --- Creates the PDF layout and triggers a browser
  download.

- **Error Banner** --- Warning indicator that lists field validation
  issues.

## **10.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Enter the recipient\'s full name (the field will automatically
capitalize the entry).

Step 2 -- Enter the Course Title and Duration (e.g., \"03 MONTHS\").

Step 3 -- Provide a unique alphanumeric Certificate ID.

Step 4 -- Choose the course enrollment Start Date and End Date.

Step 5 -- Click \'Generate & Download Certificate\' to download the PDF
document.

> **📌 Note:** Ensure the Certificate ID is unique and documented in the
> student portfolio to prevent verification conflicts.

# **11. Projects Management**

The dashboard to monitor student engagement latency and quality audits.

## **11.1 How to Access**

- Select \'Quality Audit\' under the CORE group in the left sidebar
  menu.

## **11.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **Sync Intelligence Button** --- Recalculates all quality bands, CSAT
  rates, and SLA compliances.

- **Retraining Alerts** --- Warnings about mentors with critical SLA
  breaches.

- **KPI Cards** --- Network Performance, Certified Nodes, SLA
  Compliance, and At Risk Entities.

- **Distribution Pie Charts** --- Visual representation of quality bands
  and student dropout vectors.

- **Engagement Latency Graph** --- Chart illustrating student inactivity
  intervals.

- **Audit Registry Table** --- Registry showing CSAT ratings, SLA
  percentages, placement rates, and overall scores.

## **11.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Review the Retraining Alerts banner at the top of the page for
urgent mentor issues.

Step 2 -- Check the Entity Distribution and Dropout Vectors charts for
trends.

Step 3 -- Scroll to the Audit Registry and search for a specific mentor
or center.

Step 4 -- Check the requires-retraining status in the table (marked as
REQUIRED or CLEAR).

Step 5 -- Click \'Sync Intelligence\' to refresh all calculated stats.

> **📌 Note:** Click \"Sync Intelligence\" to trigger a backend
> recalculation of all metrics based on the latest attendance and
> project submission data.

# **13. Settings & Preferences**

The personal account console for security updates and clearance reviews.

## **13.1 How to Access**

- Select \'Preferences\' option under the SYSTEM group in the left
  sidebar menu.

## **13.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **Tab Navigators** --- Student Identity and Security Protocols
  options.

- **Student Identity Card** --- Displays your official name, username,
  and level of clearance.

- **Security Form** --- Fields for Current Access Key, New Access Key,
  and Confirm New Key.

- **Initialize Button** --- Action button to save the updated security
  password.

## **13.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Select the \'Security Protocols\' tab.

Step 2 -- Input your current password in the \'Current Access Key\'
field.

Step 3 -- Enter your new desired password in the \'New Access Key\'
field.

Step 4 -- Confirm the password by typing it again.

Step 5 -- Click \'Initialize Protocol Update\' to apply the change.

> **📌 Note:** Choose a strong password containing letters, numbers, and
> special characters. Admin security is critical to preventing
> unauthorized system changes.

# **14. Logout**

The session termination routine to secure database access.

## **14.1 How to Access**

- Select \'Logout\' option at the very bottom of the left sidebar
  navigation menu.

## **14.2 Features and UI Elements**

- **Logout Menu Item** --- Sidebar trigger with exit icon.

- **Confirmation Dialog Modal** --- Popup alert with \"Confirm Logout\"
  and \"Cancel\" buttons.

## **14.3 Step-by-Step**

Step 1 -- Scroll to the bottom of the left sidebar and click \'Logout\'.

Step 2 -- Review the confirmation dialog modal.

Step 3 -- Click \'Confirm Logout\' to clear cookies and close your
session.

Step 4 -- The system will redirect you to the Login screen.

> **📌 Note:** Always log out of the admin panel when accessing the
> system from shared or public computers to maintain database security.
